Overview
These notes rewrite the particle-mechanics sheet on probability amplitudes, normalization, detectors and measurement, infinite-square-well states, operators for momentum and energy, and one-dimensional scattering with reflection and transmission.

Core rewrite
Quantum mechanics predicts probabilities from wave amplitudes. The probability of finding a particle in a spatial interval is
$$P(a\le x\le b)=\int_a^b |\psi(x)|^2\,dx,$$
and normalization requires
$$\int |\psi(x)|^2\,dx = 1.$$
A common bound-state example is the sinusoidal infinite-well wavefunction
$$\psi(x)=A\sin(kx).$$

Operators
The momentum operator is
$$\hat p=-i\hbar\frac{d}{dx},$$
and the energy operator is
$$\hat E=i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}.$$
The notes emphasize that measurement outcomes are probabilistic and that detector clicks are linked to the square magnitude of the wavefunction.

Scattering section
For a scattering problem the incident and reflected waves in region 1 are written as
$$\psi_1=Ae^{ikx}+Be^{-ikx},$$
while region 2 contains a transmitted wave such as
$$\psi_2=Ce^{ik'x}.$$
Reflection and transmission probabilities are
$$R=|r|^2,$$
$$T=|t|^2\frac{k'}{k}.$$

Counting note
The page also briefly contrasts bosonic and fermionic occupation counting:
$$N_F=0 \text{ or } 1,\qquad N_B=0,1,2,\dots.$$
