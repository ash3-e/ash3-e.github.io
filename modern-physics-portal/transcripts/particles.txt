Particles
Overview
This sheet combines several short particle-quantum-mechanics themes: probability amplitudes, detector clicks, normalization, scattering or boundary matching, and particle counting for bosons and fermions. It reads like a bridge from wavefunctions to laboratory detection.

Probability and normalization
The basic probability rule is
$$P(a\le x\le b)=\int_a^b |\psi(x)|^2\,dx,$$
with normalization
$$\int |\psi(x)|^2 \, dx = 1.$$
The page appears to stress that $\psi$ itself is a probability amplitude, not directly a classical particle density.

Detector language
The note visibly talks about detectors, photographic or avalanche-style amplification, and macroscopic registration. One of the main pedagogical points is that a microscopic amplitude distribution produces discrete detection events, and the distribution of many such events reproduces the Born probability law.

State counting and examples
The sheet also seems to mix in bound-state examples such as the infinite square well and simple boundary matching. That naturally sets up the contrast between single-particle wavefunctions and many-particle counting language, including bosonic versus fermionic occupancy.
