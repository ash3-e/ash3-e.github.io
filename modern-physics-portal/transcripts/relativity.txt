Relativity
Overview
This is a compact introductory relativity sheet. The page contrasts the Newtonian picture with Einstein's postulates, then builds up time dilation, simultaneity shifts, and velocity-addition reasoning from simple light-clock or signal-exchange arguments.

Kinematic starting point
In the Newtonian limit one writes
$$\Delta x = \Delta x' + v\Delta t', \qquad \Delta t = \Delta t'.$$
The point of the note is that these relations fail if all inertial observers must agree on the same light speed. That forces one to replace them with Lorentz-style transformations and the associated factor $\gamma$.

Light-clock logic
The page likely uses a light-bounce argument: because the moving clock forces the light to trace a longer path in another frame, the elapsed time between ticks increases. That is the clean route to time dilation and then to simultaneity effects.

Conceptual pivot
The important theme is that measurements of space and time are frame dependent while the speed of light and the causal light-cone structure remain universal.
