Diffusion And Drift
Overview
These notes connect random microscopic motion to macroscopic transport. The page starts from random-walk intuition, builds the diffusion equation, then adds an electric-field-driven drift velocity to obtain current, mobility, and conductivity.

Diffusion
The basic random-walk scaling is
$$\langle x^2 \rangle = 2Dt,$$
and the diffusive flux is
$$J_{\text{diff}} = -D \nabla n.$$
Conservation of particle number gives the diffusion equation
$$\frac{\partial n}{\partial t} = D \nabla^2 n.$$

Drift
In an applied field the page is pointing toward a drift current of the form
$$\mathbf{J}_{\text{drift}} = q n \mu \mathbf{E},$$
together with the conductivity relation
$$\sigma = q n \mu.$$
So the note is combining unbiased spreading with directed transport in a single picture.

Physical message
Diffusion comes from gradients and random motion even without a force. Drift comes from a biasing field. In conductors and semiconductors both mechanisms are present, so the net current is the sum of the diffusive and drift pieces. The note also seems to gesture toward the Einstein relation between $D$ and $\mu$.
