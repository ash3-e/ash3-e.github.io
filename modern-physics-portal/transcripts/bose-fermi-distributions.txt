Bose-fermi Distributions — plaintext transcript

Overview
This sheet compares bosons and fermions through counting rules, equilibrium distributions, density of states, detailed balance, and condensed-matter applications such as Cooper pairs, superconductivity, semiconductors, and doped materials.

Core rewrite
Bosons can occupy the same quantum state in unrestricted numbers,
$$N_B = 0,1,2,\dots,$$
while fermions obey the Pauli exclusion principle,
$$N_F = 0 \text{ or } 1.$$
At thermal equilibrium the occupation numbers are
$$f_B(E)=\frac{1}{e^{(E-\mu)/k_B T}-1}$$
for bosons and
$$f_F(E)=\frac{1}{e^{(E-\mu)/k_B T}+1}$$
for fermions. In the dilute or classical limit both reduce approximately to Maxwell–Boltzmann form
$$f_{\text{MB}}(E)\approx e^{-(E-\mu)/k_B T}.$$

The number of states in an energy interval is built from the density of states:
$$N(E)\,dE = D(E) f(E)\,dE.$$
Transition rates carry enhancement or blocking factors. Bosonic final states are stimulated by existing occupation, whereas fermionic final states are suppressed by exclusion:
$$W_{1\to2}\propto N_1(1\pm N_2).$$
Detailed balance states that forward and reverse processes match in equilibrium.

Physical interpretation
The notes connect the distributions to low-temperature fermion filling near the Fermi energy, bosonic condensation tendencies, and the formation of Cooper pairs in superconductors. They also discuss semiconductors, p-type and n-type doping, conduction and valence bands, and the way carrier occupation depends on temperature and available states.
