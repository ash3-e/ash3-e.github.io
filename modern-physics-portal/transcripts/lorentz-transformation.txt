Lorentz Transformation
Overview
These notes focus on the Lorentz transformation as the correct replacement for Galilean kinematics when the invariant speed is $c$. The page combines interval arguments, Minkowski-diagram intuition, simultaneity shifts, and relativistic energy-momentum bookkeeping.

Coordinate changes
The main transformation relations are of the standard form
$$\Delta x=\gamma(\Delta x' + v\,\Delta t'), \qquad
\Delta t=\gamma\left(\Delta t' + \frac{v}{c^2}\Delta x'\right),$$
with inverse relations obtained by sending $v\to -v$. The factor
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}}$$
controls time dilation, length contraction, and simultaneity offsets.

Physical meaning
The page is not just quoting formulas. It is using them to compare equal-time slices in different frames and to show that events simultaneous in one frame are generally not simultaneous in another. That is the source of classic train, clock, and signal problems.

Invariant structure
A second theme is invariance: the spacetime interval and the light cone keep the same physical content in every inertial frame. So the Lorentz transformation is the statement of how different observers preserve the same causal and geometric structure.
