Bohemian Pilot Waves
Overview
These notes treat Bohmian mechanics as a hidden-variable alternative to ordinary collapse language. The page seems to compare Bohmian pilot waves with spontaneous-collapse ideas and with Many-Worlds, then rewrites the Schrödinger equation in polar form so that probability flow and phase dynamics appear as separate equations.

Core construction
Write the wavefunction in polar form:
$$\psi = R e^{iS/\hbar}.$$
Then the density is
$$\rho = R^2 = |\psi|^2.$$
Splitting the Schrödinger equation into real and imaginary parts gives a continuity equation
$$\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot \left( \rho \frac{\nabla S}{m} \right) = 0,$$
together with a Hamilton--Jacobi-like phase equation
$$\frac{\partial S}{\partial t} + \frac{(\nabla S)^2}{2m} + U + Q = 0,$$
where the quantum potential is
$$Q = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\nabla^2 R}{R}.$$

Physical picture
The note is using the standard Bohmian reading that both the wave and the particle position are physically real. The guidance law is
$$\mathbf{v} = \frac{\nabla S}{m}, \qquad \mathbf{p} = \nabla S.$$
Measurement probabilities are still summarized by the Born rule
$$P(\text{measurement})=|\psi|^2,$$
but collapse is interpreted effectively rather than as a fundamental random jump of the ontology.

Comparisons and criticisms
The page also appears to compare Bohmian mechanics to spontaneous-collapse models and Many-Worlds. It flags familiar concerns: difficulty extending the theory cleanly to relativistic situations, awkwardness for photons or phonons, the appearance of a preferred slicing or preferred frame in some formulations, and strange trajectory behavior near nodes.

Measurement examples on the same sheet
The lower part of the page appears to return to polarization and spin chains. The point there is that successive measurements depend on basis choice: inserting an intermediate 45 degree analyzer changes the surviving state and changes the final transmitted fraction. So the page is connecting interpretation talk back to standard Born-rule laboratory examples.
