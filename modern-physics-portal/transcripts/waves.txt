Waves — plaintext transcript

Overview
These notes rewrite the wave-mechanics sheet on multidimensional plane waves, wavevectors, polarization, phase and group velocity, de Broglie relations, the Schrödinger equation, interference, diffraction, and standing-wave boundary conditions.

Core rewrite
A general plane wave is written as
$$\psi(\mathbf{r},t)=A\cos(\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}-\omega t+\phi),$$
where
$$\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}=k_xx+k_yy+k_zz.$$
The wavelength is tied to the wavevector magnitude by
$$\lambda=\frac{2\pi}{|\mathbf{k}|}.$$
Phase and group velocity are distinguished by
$$v_p=\frac{\omega}{k}, \qquad v_g=\frac{d\omega}{dk}.$$

Electromagnetic and matter waves
Polarization is described with field components such as
$$\mathbf{E}=(E_x,E_y,E_z).$$
The de Broglie relations identify matter-wave parameters through
$$p=\hbar k,\qquad E=\hbar\omega.$$
This leads into the Schrödinger equation,
$$i\hbar\frac{\partial \psi}{\partial t}=-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2\psi+U\psi.$$

Classical wave equation and interference
The classical wave equation is
$$\frac{\partial^2 y}{\partial x^2}=\frac{1}{v^2}\frac{\partial^2 y}{\partial t^2}.$$
The notes also discuss superposition, constructive and destructive interference, Fraunhofer-style far-field ideas, standing-wave boundary conditions, and quantized modes on strings or in boxes.
