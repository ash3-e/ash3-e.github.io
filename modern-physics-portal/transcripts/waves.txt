Waves
Overview
These notes start from ordinary classical waves and then use the same language to motivate matter waves and the Schrödinger picture. The page treats the wavevector, polarization, dispersion, and the difference between phase and group motion.

Basic wave form
A traveling wave is written as
$$\psi(\mathbf{r},t)=A\cos(\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}-\omega t+\phi),$$
where
$$\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r} = k_x x + k_y y + k_z z, \qquad
\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{|\mathbf{k}|}.$$
The page clearly wants the reader to think geometrically about wavefronts, propagation direction, and wavelength.

Velocities and matter waves
The phase velocity is
$$v_p = \frac{\omega}{k},$$
while packet propagation is governed by
$$v_g = \frac{d\omega}{dk}.$$
The note then bridges to de Broglie relations,
$$p = \hbar k, \qquad E = \hbar \omega,$$
so that the Schrödinger equation can be read as a wave equation for matter amplitudes rather than only for classical fields.

Polarization and interference
The sheet also appears to discuss vector fields, polarization directions, path differences, constructive and destructive interference, and standing-wave boundary conditions. So the page is doing double duty: classical wave mechanics on the surface, quantum interpretation underneath.
