Overview
These notes rewrite the sheet on relativistic quantum mechanics, the Dirac equation, matrix anticommutation relations, intrinsic spin, angular momentum commutators, spin measurements, and the emergence of antimatter from the negative-energy structure of the theory.

Core rewrite
The relativistic invariant relation is
$$E^2=(pc)^2+(mc^2)^2.$$
Dirac sought an equation first order in time that still reproduces this invariant, leading to
$$i\hbar\frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t}=\left(c\,\boldsymbol{\alpha}\cdot\mathbf{p}+\beta mc^2\right)\Psi.$$
The matrices satisfy anticommutation rules
$$\{\alpha_i,\alpha_j\}=2\delta_{ij}I,\qquad \{\alpha_i,\beta\}=0,\qquad \beta^2=I.$$

Spin structure
Spin is an intrinsic angular momentum with allowed z-values
$$S_z=\pm \frac{\hbar}{2}.$$
The notes also review orbital angular momentum,
$$L_x=yp_z-zp_y,$$
with commutator algebra such as
$$[L_x,L_y]=i\hbar L_z.$$
The wavefunction can acquire sign or phase structure under rotations and periodic boundary conditions:
$$\Psi(x+2\pi r)=\Psi(x).$$

Antimatter section
The negative-energy sea picture and hole interpretation are referenced as historical motivation for antiparticles. The notes summarize the charge reversal relation
$$Q_{\text{antiparticle}}=-Q_{\text{particle}}.$$
They also connect spin, Pauli matrices, measurement projection, and the role of two-valued spin states in fermionic matter.
