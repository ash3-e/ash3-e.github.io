Spin - Antimatter
Overview
These notes tie together relativistic quantum mechanics, intrinsic spin, and the appearance of antimatter in the Dirac theory. The page uses the relativistic energy-momentum relation as the starting point and then motivates the matrix structure of the Dirac equation.

Relativistic structure
The basic dispersion relation is
$$E^2 = (pc)^2 + (mc^2)^2.$$
Linearizing that relation in momentum leads to the Dirac equation
$$i\hbar\frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t} = \left(c\,\boldsymbol{\alpha}\cdot\mathbf{p} + \beta mc^2\right)\Psi,$$
together with anticommutation conditions such as
$$\{\alpha_i,\alpha_j\} = 2\delta_{ij}I.$$

Spin content
Spin is intrinsic angular momentum, not literal mechanical spinning of a small rigid object. The multi-component Dirac spinor automatically carries spin information and predicts the right magnetic-moment-type coupling in the nonrelativistic limit.

Antimatter content
The positive- and negative-energy branches of the relativistic theory are what historically point toward antimatter. The note is likely emphasizing that antiparticles arise naturally from the relativistic quantum equation rather than being added by hand.
