Fourier Series - Transform -Wave Addition — plaintext transcript

Overview
These notes cover decomposition of functions into sinusoidal modes, Fourier transforms, Gaussian wave packets, k-space versus real space, delta functions, and the role of superposition in wave mechanics and probability amplitudes.

Core rewrite
A periodic function can be expanded as a Fourier series,
$$f(x)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left[a_n\cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)+b_n\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)\right].$$
The coefficients are obtained by projecting onto cosine and sine basis functions:
$$a_n=\frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{L}f(x)\cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)\,dx,$$
$$b_n=\frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^{L}f(x)\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)\,dx.$$

For nonperiodic functions the Fourier transform is used:
$$F(k)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x)e^{-ikx}\,dx,$$
with inverse
$$f(x)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} F(k)e^{ikx}\,dk.$$

Wave-packet interpretation
A Gaussian packet in real space transforms into a Gaussian in k-space. Narrow localization in real space makes the spread in k-space large, while a narrow spread in k-space gives a broad spatial packet. The notes also point out the delta-function limit,
$$\delta(k-k_0)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int e^{i(k-k_0)x}\,dx,$$
and emphasize the uncertainty-style scaling
$$\Delta x\,\Delta k \sim 1.$$
