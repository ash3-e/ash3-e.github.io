Bra-ket - Entanglement — plaintext transcript

Overview
These notes introduce Dirac bra-ket notation, wavefunction inner products, polarization states, Malus' law, entangled photon pairs, Bell correlations, and the EPR argument against local hidden-variable explanations.

Core rewrite
A ket stores the state vector,
$$|v\rangle = (v_1, v_2, \dots)^T,$$
and the corresponding bra is its complex-conjugate transpose,
$$\langle v| = (v_1^*, v_2^*, \dots).$$
For continuous wavefunctions the inner product is
$$\langle u|v\rangle = \int u^*(x) v(x)\,dx.$$
Normalized states satisfy
$$\langle \psi | \psi \rangle = 1.$$

Linear polarization is written as a superposition of horizontal and vertical basis states,
$$\mathbf{E} = \alpha |H\rangle + \beta |V\rangle.$$
Passage through a polarizer obeys Malus' law,
$$I = I_0 \cos^2 \theta.$$

Entanglement section
A standard maximally entangled two-photon state is
$$|\Phi^+\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(|HH\rangle + |VV\rangle\right).$$
Correlations depend on analyzer settings through expressions such as
$$E(a,b)=\cos 2(a-b).$$
Bell-test combinations are formed from
$$S = E(a,b) - E(a,b') + E(a',b) + E(a',b').$$
The notes connect this with EPR reasoning and the failure of local hidden-variable models to reproduce the observed quantum correlations.
