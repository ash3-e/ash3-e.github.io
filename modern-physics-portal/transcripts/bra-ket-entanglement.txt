Bra-ket - Entanglement
Overview
This sheet uses bra-ket notation as the language for quantum states, measurement amplitudes, and entanglement. It mixes abstract vector-space notation with concrete polarization examples and Bell/EPR-style two-particle correlations.

State notation
A ket $|v\rangle$ represents a state vector and a bra $\langle v|$ is its dual. Inner products such as
$$\langle u|v\rangle = \int u^*(x) v(x)\,dx$$
or in finite dimensions
$$|v\rangle = (v_1,v_2,\dots)^T, \qquad \langle v| = (v_1^*,v_2^*,\dots)$$
encode normalization, orthogonality, and measurement amplitudes.

Entanglement content
The entanglement part of the page is naturally read in tensor-product language. A two-particle state need not factor into separate one-particle states. Bell states are special superpositions whose correlations cannot be reproduced by assigning fully independent local outcomes in advance.

Measurement logic
The page likely connects bra-ket notation to projection: choose a basis, project the state onto one of the allowed outcomes, square the amplitude to get the probability, and renormalize the surviving component after the measurement. That is the same logic behind spin-$1/2$ and polarization-filter examples.
