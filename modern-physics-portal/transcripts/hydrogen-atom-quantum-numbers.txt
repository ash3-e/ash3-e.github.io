Hydrogen Atom - Quantum Numbers — plaintext transcript

Overview
This sheet rewrites the hydrogen-atom notes linking the Coulomb potential to bound states, Bohr-scale atomic structure, orbital and spin quantum numbers, degeneracy, angular momentum, and orbital notation.

Core rewrite
The electron in hydrogen moves in the Coulomb potential
$$V(r)=-\frac{e^2}{4\pi \varepsilon_0 r}.$$
The characteristic atomic length is the Bohr radius
$$a_0=\frac{4\pi \varepsilon_0 \hbar^2}{m_e e^2}.$$
Bound-state energies are quantized as
$$E_n=-\frac{13.6\,\text{eV}}{n^2}, \qquad n=1,2,3,\dots.$$

Quantum numbers
The principal quantum number is $$n$$. For a given $$n$$ the orbital angular-momentum quantum number runs over
$$\ell = 0,1,\dots,n-1,$$
and the magnetic quantum number is
$$m_\ell = -\ell,-\ell+1,\dots,\ell.$$
Electron spin adds
$$m_s = \pm \frac12.$$

Angular momentum structure
Orbital angular momentum has magnitude
$$L^2=\ell(\ell+1)\hbar^2,$$
with z-component
$$L_z = m_\ell \hbar.$$
The spatial wavefunction is built from a radial factor and spherical harmonic:
$$\psi_{n\ell m}(r,\theta,\phi)=R_{n\ell}(r)Y_\ell^m(\theta,\phi).$$
The notes also gesture toward orbital pictures, degeneracy of states at fixed $$n$$, and the relation between quantum numbers and atomic spectroscopy.
