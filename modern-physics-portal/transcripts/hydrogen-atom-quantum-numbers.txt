Hydrogen Atom - Quantum Numbers
Overview
These notes summarize the hydrogen atom as the clean central example of quantization in a Coulomb potential. The page is organized around the bound-state spectrum, the Bohr radius, and the meaning of the quantum numbers $n$, $\ell$, and $m$.

Core scales
The potential is
$$V(r)=-\frac{e^2}{4\pi \varepsilon_0 r},$$
and the atomic length scale is the Bohr radius
$$a_0=\frac{4\pi\varepsilon_0\hbar^2}{m_e e^2}.$$
The bound-state energies are
$$E_n=-\frac{13.6\,\text{eV}}{n^2}.$$

Quantum numbers
The principal quantum number $n$ fixes the energy for pure hydrogen. The orbital quantum number $\ell$ labels angular-momentum magnitude and $m$ labels the $z$ component. The note likely emphasizes that, before fine-structure corrections, hydrogen states with the same $n$ but different $\ell$ and $m$ are degenerate.

Structural picture
The wavefunction factors into radial and angular pieces, so the page is also functioning as a compact reminder about orbitals, spherical harmonics, and the link between algebraic quantum numbers and spatial orbital shapes.
