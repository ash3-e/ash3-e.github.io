Thermodynamics - Statistical Mechanics — plaintext transcript

Overview
This page condenses the four laws of thermodynamics, entropy, the H-theorem, ideal-gas relations, heat capacity, phase-space counting, and photon or phonon energy scaling into a single statistical-mechanics summary sheet.

Core rewrite
The first-law bookkeeping is
$$dU=dQ-dW,$$
with pressure work
$$dW=PdV.$$
Entropy is introduced statistically as
$$S=k_B\ln\Omega,$$
and the second law appears as
$$\Delta S\ge 0.$$

Ideal gas and heat capacity
The ideal-gas law is
$$PV=Nk_B T,$$
and for a monatomic ideal gas
$$U=\frac32 Nk_B T.$$
Heat capacity at constant volume is
$$C_V=\left(\frac{\partial U}{\partial T}\right)_V,$$
giving the familiar constant result for the classical monatomic gas.

Statistical counting
Occupied energy states are counted through
$$N(E)\,dE=D(E)f(E)\,dE,$$
where $$D(E)$$ is the density of states and $$f(E)$$ is the occupation factor. The page also mentions Boltzmann’s H-theorem, equilibrium arguments, and phase-space or k-space counting.

Radiation and low-temperature behavior
Photon gases and phonon-like excitations are summarized by the scaling
$$U\propto T^4,$$
together with the average-particle-energy result
$$\langle E\rangle=\frac32 k_B T$$
for classical translational degrees of freedom.
